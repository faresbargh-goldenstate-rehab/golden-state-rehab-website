Questions families ask us

What if they will not admit they have a problem?

This is the most common place families start. Your loved one does not need to be fully on board for you to call us. We will talk through where they are and give you practical ways to open the conversation without it turning into a fight.

Will I still be able to stay involved?

Yes. Because care is outpatient, your loved one stays part of daily life rather than disappearing for months. With their consent, we keep families informed and involved in a way that supports recovery.

How much is this going to cost us?

Most of our clients use insurance, and we accept most major plans. We will verify your loved one's coverage and explain what it covers before anything moves forward, so there are no surprises.

What if they refuse treatment right now?

That happens, and it does not mean the door is closed. We will help you prepare so that when they are ready, even if it is at 2am, you know exactly who to call and what to say.

Is the first call really free and confidential?

Yes. The first conversation costs nothing and carries no obligation. What you share with our admissions team stays private.
