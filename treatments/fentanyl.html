Fentanyl Addiction Treatment in Los Angeles

Medically supervised, evidence-based treatment for fentanyl addiction and dependence in Los Angeles

Medically reviewed by Dr. Eric Chaghouri, MD — Medical Director, Golden State Rehab Updated June 2026
Fentanyl Use Disorder

Fentanyl Is Different — So Treatment Has to Be

Fentanyl is a synthetic opioid roughly 50 times more potent than heroin and 100 times more potent than morphine. It now dominates the illicit opioid supply in Los Angeles, and it is increasingly pressed into counterfeit pills (often sold as "M30" oxycodone or Xanax) and mixed into other drugs. That means many people develop a fentanyl dependence they never chose — and that the risk of overdose is higher than with any opioid that came before it.

Because of fentanyl's unique potency and how it lingers in the body, standard opioid protocols don't always translate one-to-one. Treatment has to be fentanyl-aware: medically supervised withdrawal, carefully managed buprenorphine (Suboxone) induction, overdose prevention, and behavioral therapy — all of which we provide through our outpatient PHP and IOP programs in West Los Angeles.

Treatment Methods

Our Fentanyl Treatment Approach

Medically Supervised Withdrawal

Fentanyl withdrawal is intense but manageable with medical support. Our clinical team monitors symptoms and uses evidence-based medications to keep you safe and as comfortable as possible while your body stabilizes.

Fentanyl-Aware Buprenorphine (Suboxone)

Buprenorphine is the most effective medication for opioid use disorder, including fentanyl. Because fentanyl stores in body tissue and can complicate a standard start, we use careful, fentanyl-aware induction protocols to avoid precipitated withdrawal.

Naltrexone (Vivitrol)

For patients who complete withdrawal and prefer a non-opioid medication, extended-release naltrexone (Vivitrol) blocks opioid receptors and removes the reward from any future use — supporting abstinence-based recovery.

Overdose Prevention & Naloxone

Every patient and family leaves knowing how to recognize and reverse an overdose. We provide naloxone (Narcan) education and harm-reduction guidance — because keeping you alive comes before everything else.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

Our CBT work targets the triggers, thoughts, and high-risk situations that drive use. You'll build concrete skills to handle cravings and the emotional states that previously led back to fentanyl.

Dual Diagnosis & Trauma Care

Fentanyl use often co-occurs with trauma, anxiety, or depression. Our dual-diagnosis approach treats the addiction and the underlying mental-health condition together, not one after the other.

What You Should Know

The Truth About Fentanyl in Los Angeles

Fear and stigma keep people from getting help. Here's what the evidence actually shows — and why recovery is absolutely possible:

  • Fentanyl is now present in most of LA's illicit opioid supply, and frequently in counterfeit pills and stimulants
  • Many people are exposed to fentanyl without knowing it — dependence is a medical condition, not a moral failing
  • Buprenorphine and naltrexone are proven to work for fentanyl use disorder
  • Medication-assisted treatment reduces overdose deaths by roughly half
  • Naloxone (Narcan) reverses a fentanyl overdose — though it may take more than one dose
  • Most people can be treated effectively in outpatient care, without leaving work or family behind
