About Telehealth

What Is Online IOP at Golden State Rehab?

Golden State Rehab's telehealth program delivers our full Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP) by secure, HIPAA-compliant video — live 3-hour sessions, 3 to 5 days per week, about 9 to 15 hours of treatment weekly, with morning and evening tracks, from anywhere in California. You keep your job and your routine while getting real clinical care. You join real-time group therapy, meet one-on-one with your primary therapist each week, and see our board-certified psychiatrist for medication support — all from a computer, tablet, or phone, with no downloads needed. It's the same evidence-based curriculum as our in-person program at our Westwood office in West Los Angeles, built for people who live farther away, can't manage the commute, or simply feel more at ease starting treatment from home. Most major insurance plans are accepted, and a free, confidential assessment determines whether online IOP is the right level of care for you.

Your Weekly Rhythm

What a Week of Online IOP Looks Like

Telehealth follows the same schedule as our in-person IOP: live 3-hour video sessions, 3–5 days a week — 9 to 15 hours in total, set at your free assessment and adjusted as you progress.

Live Group Sessions

Real-time video groups covering CBT, DBT, relapse prevention, and process work — the same therapists and the same curriculum as our Westwood program.

Morning or Evening Track

Choose the track that fits your life: morning sessions before the day fills up, or evening sessions after work — with no commute either way.

Woven Into Each Week

A private video session with your primary therapist every week, plus psychiatric consultations and medication support as your treatment plan calls for.

Work & Life

Can I Keep Working During Online IOP?

Yes — online IOP is about as work-friendly as real treatment gets. The evening track meets after typical work hours, and there's no commute and no waiting room. Log off work, log in to group.

Your privacy holds up too. We never contact your employer, and your records are protected by HIPAA and 42 CFR Part 2 — a federal confidentiality rule written specifically for substance use treatment. If you ever want time away, federal FMLA and California's CFRA generally give eligible employees the right to job-protected leave for a serious health condition. That's general legal information, not legal advice — but getting help rarely has to put your job at risk.
