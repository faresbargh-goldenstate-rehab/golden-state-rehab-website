How Much Does Rehab Cost in Los Angeles? (2026 Guide)

A clear, no-spin breakdown of what addiction and mental-health treatment actually costs in LA — by level of care — plus how insurance, parity law, and payment options change the number you actually pay.

Dr. Eric Chaghouri, MD
Medical Director
• June 5, 2026 9 min read

For most families, the scariest part of getting help isn't the treatment — it's the question they're afraid to ask out loud: how are we going to pay for this? Cost is the single biggest reason people delay or avoid rehab. So let's take the mystery out of it. Here's a straight, no-spin breakdown of what addiction and mental-health treatment actually costs in Los Angeles in 2026 — and why the sticker price is almost never what you'll really pay.

The short answer: typical rehab costs by level of care

Below are general U.S. and California price ranges for each level of care in 2026. Treat these as list prices before insurance. With in-network coverage, most people pay a small fraction of these numbers — often just a deductible or copay. These are industry ranges to orient you, not a quote; your real cost depends on your plan.

Level of CareWhat It IsTypical Range (before insurance)
Medical Detox3–7 days of 24/7 monitored withdrawal$1,000–$1,500 / day
Residential / InpatientLive-in care, usually 30–90 days$20,000–$60,000+ / month
Partial Hospitalization (PHP)Day program, ~5–6 hrs/day, 5 days/week$7,000–$12,000 / month
Intensive Outpatient (IOP)9–15 structured hours per week$3,000–$7,000 / month
Standard OutpatientWeekly therapy + medication management$1,000–$3,000 / month
Sober LivingStructured recovery housing$800–$2,500 / month

Golden State Rehab is an outpatient provider — our PHP, IOP, and telehealth programs sit in the lower-cost tiers above, while delivering the same evidence-based clinical care as far more expensive residential programs.

The number that actually matters Your out-of-pocket cost — not the list price. With in-network insurance, what you actually pay is usually your plan's deductible, copay, or coinsurance. That can bring a full course of outpatient treatment down to a few hundred dollars, and sometimes to $0 once your deductible is met.

What actually drives the cost of rehab

Two people can pay wildly different amounts for "rehab." Here's what moves the number:

  • Level of care — residential costs far more than outpatient because it includes 24/7 housing, food, and staffing.
  • Length of treatment — a 90-day program naturally costs more than 30 days.
  • Your insurance — in-network coverage is the single biggest factor. It often turns a five-figure list price into a modest copay.
  • Clinical complexity — dual-diagnosis care (treating addiction and a mental-health condition together) may involve psychiatry and medication management.
  • Location & amenities — luxury, beachfront, or executive programs charge a premium that has little to do with clinical outcomes.

Does insurance cover rehab? Almost always — here's why

Yes. Under the federal Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) and the Affordable Care Act, mental-health and substance-use treatment are essential health benefits. In plain English: insurers must cover addiction and mental-health care at parity with medical and surgical care — they can't impose higher copays, stricter limits, or tougher approval just because it's behavioral health.

In practice, most major plans — Aetna, Cigna, Anthem Blue Cross, Blue Shield of California, UnitedHealthcare, Kaiser, and more — cover outpatient addiction and mental-health treatment. The coverage details (deductible, copay, in- vs out-of-network) vary by plan, which is exactly why a benefits check matters more than any price chart.

Ways to pay if you're uninsured or underinsured

  • In-network insurance — the lowest-cost path for most people. Verify your benefits to see your real number.
  • Out-of-network benefits — many PPO plans still reimburse a portion even out-of-network.
  • Sliding-scale & payment plans — many providers, including us, work with families on affordability.
  • HSA / FSA funds — treatment is a qualified medical expense.
  • Scholarships & grants — some nonprofits and facilities offer need-based aid.
  • Free national resources — the SAMHSA National Helpline (1-800-662-4357) offers free, confidential referrals 24/7.

How much does rehab cost in Los Angeles specifically?

LA generally sits at the higher end of national ranges because of cost of living — especially on the Westside (Santa Monica, Beverly Hills, Brentwood, Westwood). But there's an enormous spread: a luxury residential stay in Malibu can run six figures, while high-quality outpatient care in the same city is a fraction of that. The clinical evidence is clear that outpatient programs like PHP and IOP produce strong outcomes for most people — so for many Angelenos, the most effective option is also the most affordable. Frequently asked questions

Does insurance cover rehab in California?

In most cases, yes. Under federal parity law and the ACA, California health plans must cover addiction and mental-health treatment as essential health benefits. The exact amount depends on your plan — verify your benefits to confirm.

What's the difference in cost between PHP and IOP?

PHP is more intensive (about 5–6 hours a day) and therefore costs more than IOP (about 9–15 hours a week). Both are far less expensive than residential care, and both are commonly covered by insurance.

Can I go to rehab with no money or no insurance?

Yes. Options include sliding-scale fees, payment plans, HSA/FSA funds, scholarships, and free referral support through the SAMHSA National Helpline (1-800-662-4357). Don't let cost stop you from making the call — there is almost always a path.

Is outpatient rehab as effective as inpatient?

For many people, yes. Research shows outpatient programs deliver strong outcomes for a wide range of conditions, while letting you keep working, studying, and living at home — at a fraction of residential cost.
