Does Medi-Cal Cover Rehab in California? (2026 Guide)

Yes — Medi-Cal covers addiction and mental-health treatment through Drug Medi-Cal. Here's what's covered, who qualifies, and exactly how to find Medi-Cal rehab in Los Angeles.

Dr. Eric Chaghouri, MD
Medical Director
• June 6, 2026 9 min read

If you don't have private insurance, it's easy to assume rehab is out of reach. For millions of Californians, it isn't — because Medi-Cal, the state's Medicaid program, covers addiction and mental-health treatment. You don't need a private PPO or a fat bank account to get real, evidence-based care. You may already have the coverage you need and not realize it.

This guide explains how Medi-Cal covers rehab in plain language: what's included, who qualifies, and the exact steps to find Medi-Cal addiction treatment in Los Angeles. (If you have private insurance instead, see our companion guide on whether insurance covers rehab in California.)

The short answer: yes, Medi-Cal covers rehab

Medi-Cal covers substance-use disorder treatment through a program called Drug Medi-Cal — and in most counties, an expanded version called the Drug Medi-Cal Organized Delivery System (DMC-ODS). It also covers mental-health services. Because addiction and mental-health treatment are essential health benefits under federal law, Medi-Cal treats them as core covered care — not an optional add-on.

The key point Medi-Cal members generally pay little or nothing out of pocket for covered substance-use and mental-health treatment. Cost is rarely the barrier — knowing where to go is. This guide fixes that.

What Medi-Cal covers for addiction treatment

Through Drug Medi-Cal / DMC-ODS, covered services typically include the full continuum of care:

  • Outpatient and intensive outpatient (IOP) treatment
  • Withdrawal management (medically supervised detox)
  • Residential treatment (when medically necessary)
  • Medication-assisted treatment (MAT) — including buprenorphine, methadone, and naltrexone
  • Recovery services — counseling, relapse prevention, and peer support after primary treatment
  • Case management to coordinate your care
  • Mental-health services for co-occurring conditions like depression, anxiety, and trauma

The specific level of care you receive is based on clinical need, assessed using the nationally recognized ASAM Criteria — the same standard used by private insurers in California.

Who qualifies for Medi-Cal?

Medi-Cal is available to California residents who meet income requirements. Thanks to the Affordable Care Act's expansion, eligibility is broader than many people expect — many low-income adults qualify even without children or a disability. Key facts:

  • Eligibility is based mainly on household income relative to the federal poverty level.
  • You can apply year-round (there's no limited enrollment window for Medi-Cal).
  • Apply online through Covered California or DHCS / BenefitsCal, by phone, or in person at your county social services office.
  • If you're approved, coverage can be retroactive in some situations — so don't let a pending application stop you from seeking help.

How Medi-Cal substance-use treatment is organized

Medi-Cal behavioral health is run at the county level. Each participating county operates its own Drug Medi-Cal system and a mental-health plan. That means the exact provider network and intake process vary by county — but the covered benefits are consistent statewide. Los Angeles County participates in the DMC-ODS, so LA residents on Medi-Cal have access to the full continuum of covered addiction services.

How to find Medi-Cal rehab in Los Angeles

Here's the fastest path to covered treatment:

  • Call the SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 — free, confidential, 24/7, in English and Spanish. They provide referrals to state-funded and Medi-Cal treatment programs.
  • Call the member services number on your Medi-Cal (BIC) card and ask for substance-use disorder or behavioral-health services.
  • Contact your county behavioral health department. In Los Angeles, the county operates a Substance Abuse Service Helpline for Medi-Cal members seeking treatment and bed availability.
  • Visit the DHCS Medi-Cal website to find participating providers and learn about your benefits.

Medi-Cal vs. private insurance: what's the difference?

Both cover addiction and mental-health treatment. The practical differences:

  • Cost: Medi-Cal members usually pay little to nothing; private plans involve deductibles, copays, and coinsurance.
  • Network: Medi-Cal care is delivered through county-contracted providers; private PPO plans often allow more provider choice.
  • Access: Both are protected by parity laws, but private plans may offer shorter waits at some specialized programs.

If your situation changes — for example, you get a job with health benefits — you can transition from Medi-Cal to private coverage. To understand the typical price tags either way, see our cost of rehab in Los Angeles guide.

Don't have Medi-Cal yet? Don't wait to get help

If you're uninsured and not yet enrolled, you can still reach out for help today. The SAMHSA National Helpline (1-800-662-4357) can connect you with free and low-cost options while your Medi-Cal application is processed. Many programs will help you apply for Medi-Cal as part of intake. The most important step is the first one — making contact.

Frequently asked questions

Does Medi-Cal cover rehab in California?

Yes. Medi-Cal covers substance-use disorder treatment through Drug Medi-Cal / DMC-ODS, including outpatient care, intensive outpatient (IOP), withdrawal management, residential treatment when medically necessary, and medication-assisted treatment — plus mental-health services.

Does Medi-Cal cover outpatient and IOP treatment?

Yes. Outpatient and intensive outpatient services are core covered benefits under Drug Medi-Cal. The level of care is determined by clinical need using the ASAM Criteria.

Does Medi-Cal cover detox and medication-assisted treatment (MAT)?

Yes. Medi-Cal covers medically supervised withdrawal management and MAT medications such as buprenorphine, methadone, and naltrexone for opioid and alcohol use disorders.

How do I find a Medi-Cal rehab near me in Los Angeles?

Call the SAMHSA National Helpline at 1-800-662-4357 for free referrals, call the member services number on your Medi-Cal card, or contact the Los Angeles County behavioral health helpline. Los Angeles County participates in the DMC-ODS, so a full range of covered services is available.

Is there a cost for rehab with Medi-Cal?

Medi-Cal members generally pay little to nothing for covered substance-use and mental-health treatment. Cost is rarely the barrier for Medi-Cal members.

Can I switch from Medi-Cal to private insurance later?

Yes. If your circumstances change — for example, you gain employer coverage — you can move to a private plan. Parity protections apply to both, so addiction and mental-health treatment remain covered.

The bottom line

Medi-Cal covers rehab. If you or someone you love is struggling and money has felt like the wall in the way, that wall is lower than you think. Make one call — to SAMHSA, your county, or the number on your card — and take the first step today.
